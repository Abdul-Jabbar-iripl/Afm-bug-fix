Session 10.1

Testing Frequencies

Nozzles & Couplers

  • Underwing and Overwing nozzles shall be checked at Yearly intervals as for general condition and operation.
  • Each intake coupler shall be checked for general condition and operation at Yearly intervals.

10.1.1 Hose-end strainers

  • Wire mesh strainers fitted to pressure couplings (underwing nozzles) and overwing nozzles shall be removed and inspected monthly.
  • Where the hose-end coupling has to be disassembled to remove the strainer for inspection, the integrity of the coupling shall be checked by pressurising the hose to working pressure after reassembly.
  • A hose-end strainer (not coarser than 60 mesh) shall be fitted to pressure fuelling couplings and overwing fuelling nozzles.
  • For equipment fitted with filter monitors, the hose-end strainer shall be 100 mesh

Hose-end strainers

  • Under normal operating conditions, nozzle screens shall be removed and cleaned at Monthly intervals.
  • Except if a routine inspection reveals an increased quantity of particles, the Hose-end strainers on the subject equipment shall then be inspected at weekly intervals until three (3) consecutive inspections have shown that the source of the particles has been removed.

10.1.2 Underwing Nozzles

  • General
  • Hose-end strainers
  • Nozzle Inspection

Underwing Nozzles

  • Nozzles and couplers shall be checked for leaks during every fuelling operation and shall be checked for wear at least annually using the appropriate gauge(s) (and/or instructions) provided or approved by the equipment manufacturer.

Underwing Nozzles

  • Nozzles and inlet couplers shall be provided with storage brackets or hangers which will retain the nozzle securely during movement of the unit in which are easily accessible and simple to operate.
  • Storage brackets and hangers shall be installed in such a position that the hose is not forced or kinked in the stored position.

10.1.2.1 Underwing Nozzles Hose-end strainers Inspection

  • Nozzles and hoses shall be positioned to ensure that product is flushed through the strainer before it is removed for inspection; i.e., ensure that the product is not back flushed through the strainer before removing it for inspection.

Underwing Nozzles Hose-end strainers Inspection

Underwing Nozzles Hose-end strainers Inspection

  • Step 1: Empty hose through a proper receptacle that allows the valve to open and provides locking action in such a position
  • Step 2: Take away the safety wire and unscrew nozzle coupling bolts
  • Step 3: Remove carefully the retainer ring with the nozzle on receptacle to contain any remaining fuel
  • Step 4: Remove Strainer carefully and make a visual inspection on any particle or solid found on screen. Look specially for hose lining or rubber pieces that can indicate Hose dismantling

1. Strainer Cleaning Procedure

    Some of the debris captured may not be visible to the human eye, and therefore even a seemingly clean hose-end strainer shall still be cleaned per the below procedure

  1. Invert hose-end strainer and tap it on a clean surface to dislodge and examine any contents (dirty side down)
  2. Any debris found shall be documented
  3. Completely submerge into the bonded bucket of clean fuel
  4. Vigorously agitate the hose-end strainer while submersed in the bucket of fuel of the same grade for about 1 minute. Take care not to splash any fuel outside of the bucket
  5. While keeping the hose-end strainer below the top level of the container, successively lift and lower the hose-end strainer into and out of the fuel to back flush any debris into the fuel bucket
  6. Perform this process at least 10 times
  7. After agitating the hose-end strainer in fuel as described above, at least one of the below procedures shall be followed to complete the cleaning process
  8. Regardless of the chosen option choses, rinse the strainer in fresh fuel before returning the strainer to service

2. Compressed Air Cleaning Method

  1. Using the compressed air, blow any potential debris from the “clean side” of the hose-end strainer towards the “dirty” or “upstream” side of the strainer.
    1. Ensure that the small jet of air passes over the entire surface area of the hose-end strainer cone
    2. Repeat the air cleaning process at least three times over the entire exterior surface area of the hose-end strainer
  2. Verify hose-end strainer cleanliness and repeat cleaning if necessary
  3. Dispose of the fuel and any contaminants within the bucket according to the site’s hazardous waste disposal processes
  4. For fueling equipment with monitor Fuel shall not be returned to the vehicle product recovery tank as it could contain Super Absorbent Polymer or other fuel contaminants

Precautions

  • A lubricant-free compressed air system shall be used
  • The use of compressed air systems shall be treated with care and following site work control procedures
  • Exercise caution and avoid creating mists

3. Brush Cleaning Method

  1. Using a fine stiff haired 1/4” brush, stipple the downstream side of the hose-end strainer to loosen any potential debris pushing it towards the upstream side of the hose-end strainer. Be sure to cover the entire exterior of the hose-end strainer with the stippling process
  2. Wet the brush using clean fuel
  3. Starting from the interior point of the hose-end strainer, use the brush to carefully brush any debris towards the rim and out of the hose-end strainer. Re-wet the brush frequently in clean fuel to aid the cleaning/flushing process. Ensure that no brush bristles are dislodged and get caught in the hose-end strainer
  4. Verify hose-end strainer cleanliness and repeat cleaning if necessary
  5. Dispose of the fuel and any contaminants within the bucket according to the site’s hazardous waste disposal processes
  6. For fueling equipment with monitor fuel shall not be returned to the vehicle product recovery tank as it could contain Super Absorbent Polymer or other fuel contaminants

Underwing Nozzles Hose-end strainers Inspection

  • The Hose-end strainers should be made of minimum 60 mesh stainless steel and 100 mesh for equipment fitted with filter monitors
  • The source of any material found on the screens shall be investigated
  • The material found may be from the following sources:
    1. Pieces of Rubber indicating hoses may be deteriorating
    2. Fibers indicating deterioration of filter/separator elements. These may appear shortly after new elements are installed but should normally stop after a short period. If presence of fibers continues, particularly in larger quantities, the filter/separator elements shall be checked
    3. Metal particles indicating possible deterioration of swivel joints, meters or other components
  • Note that one way of checking for hose rubber is by burning the pieces and checking the odor

10.1.2.2 Underwing Nozzles Inspection

  • All maintenance on nozzles or hose end controllers shall be performed strictly in accordance with the manufacturers’ instructions and using only spare parts supplied or approved by the manufacturers.

Underwing Nozzles Inspection

  • Nose seals shall be inspected carefully for condition, proper alignment and position of retaining ring.
  • The proper type and model nose seals shall be used on nozzles since the sealing of the coupler to the adapter depends on adequate compression of the nozzle seal.

Underwing Nozzles Inspection

  • Hose-end strainers gaskets shall be checked for condition and, if necessary, replaced.
  • Nozzle caps and retaining cables shall be inspected for general condition.

Underwing Nozzles Inspection

  • Nozzle swivels shall be lubricated if there is any evidence of binding. Lubricant should be white petroleum jelly used lightly.
  • The atmospheric vent screens of hose end controllers shall be checked to ensure that they are clear and without any tell-tale leaks.

Underwing Nozzles Inspection

  • Nozzles equipped with opening and closing levers shall be checked to ensure the lever travels to the proper over center position and that the proper interlock operation is obtained by attempting to remove the nozzle in the valve open position.

Underwing Nozzles Inspection

  • All nozzles shall be checked for security of components, proper engagement on a dummy aircraft adapter, distortion, poppet height adjustment and correct assembly in accordance with the manufacturers’ instructions.
  • Engagement slots shall be checked for wear with the manufacturers’ wear gauge.
  • This gauge or a separate gauge should also be used for checking wear on aircraft adapters if excessive wear is suspected.

Underwing Nozzles Inspection

  • Assembly shall include proper replacement of all retaining devices, screws, lock wiring, rings, etc. to check the nozzle is ready to be tested.
  • Whenever any reassembly of a nozzle is completed (for example, after a strainer inspection), product shall be circulated through the hose for at least one (1) minute at a high flow rate but with some back pressure to confirm correct reassembly and no leaks.

10.1.3 Overwing Nozzles

  • Overwing Nozzle Inspection

Overwing Nozzles

  • Overwing nozzles: Overwing nozzles shall be checked for general condition and leaks during every fuelling operation. Records of repairs and adjustments shall be maintained

Overwing Nozzle Inspection

  • Overwing nozzles shall be checked as follows:
    1. Check that Overwing (trigger) fuelling nozzles shall not have hold-open ratchets
    2. Remove spout and check strainer, ensure strainer is undamaged and check its contents for any signs of upstream problems. Ensure strainer seals are in good condition

Overwing Nozzle Inspection

  • Check the dust cap is in good condition and attached properly

10.1.4 Intake Hose Couplers

  • Intake Hose Couplers Internal Parts

Intake Hose Couplers

  • Each intake coupler shall be checked as follows:

Intake Hose Couplers

  • Bumper guards shall be carefully inspected for condition, proper alignment and correct seating in the groove.

Intake Hose Couplers

  • Dust caps and retaining cables, or cradles, shall be inspected for general condition.

Intake Hose Couplers

  • Swivels shall be lubricated if there is any evidence of binding
  • All maintenance on intake hose couplers shall be performed strictly in accordance with the manufacturers' instructions and using manufacturers' wear gauges and items supplied or approved by the manufacturer if spares are required
  • Assembly shall include proper replacement of all retaining devices, screws, rings, etc. to check the coupler is ready to be tested

Intake Hose Couplers

  • The integrity of any reassembly work shall be confirmed by circulating against some back pressure for at least one (1) minute and checking for leaks.

10.1.5

  • Nozzle holders shall be inspected regularly for general condition and proper operation.

Nozzle Holders

  • Ensure that the action of placing/removing the nozzle in the holder operates the brakes interlock switch properly.
  • Check the latching arrangement holding the nozzle in position must ensure no intermittent operation occurs.

Nozzle Holders

  • Loose or improperly adjusted nozzle holders can cause inadvertent activation of the brake interlock system which could cause a serious vehicular accident.
