Session 12.12

Deadman Control

  • Deadman control are required to be fitted on all pressure-fueling vehicles
  • Deadman control is used to START the fuel flow at the beginning of a fuelling operation and is used to STOP the flow at any time during the fueling operation
  • Deadman is a safety device to STOP the FUEL FLOW QUICKLY and easily in an emergency
  • Refuellers - deadman operates on the downstream side of the delivery pump
  • Hydrant Systems - deadman activates valve closure upstream of the hydrant dispenser inlet hose
  • Deadman control should open the fuel valve SLOWLY and PROGRESSIVELY to avoid imposing pressure on the aircraft fuel system
  • It should take a minimum of 5 seconds from the start of flow to full flow or at least 3 seconds if the maximum flow rate is less than 2,000 LPM
  • It should STOP fuel flow within 2 to 5 seconds of releasing the deadman device
  • Closure FASTER than 2 seconds may cause excessive upstream shock pressures that could damage vehicle components
  • Deadman control shall have intermittent “TIMER” requiring PERIODIC ACTION by the operator (not exceeding 2 minutes) to prevent automatic close-down
  • When using CORDLESS deadman operator shall remain within 20 metres and LINE OF SIGHT of the fuelling vehicle during the operation
  • Deadman Override is installed to complete the fueling operation in the event of failure of the handheld Deadman Control
  • There are two types of Deadman override (i) a PUSH-BUTTON type (ii) a SWITCH type
  • Push Button requires the operator to push and hold the button in the depressed position to activate the deadman system and maintain the fuel flow
  • Switch which overrides the deadman control system shall be sealed
  • Cordless Deadman designs with a hose-reel rewind control should NOT be used because of the risk of inadvertent activation during fuelling
