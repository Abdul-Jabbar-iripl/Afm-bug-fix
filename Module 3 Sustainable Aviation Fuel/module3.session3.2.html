A GENERAL OVERVIEW OF CLIMATE CHANGE

    HOW DOES SAF CONTRIBUTE TO THE REDUCTION IN GHG EMISSIONS?
  • Reduction in GHG emissions is achieved through holistic accounting of all emissions associated with the fuel, including its production methods and feedstock source.
  • Carbon from fossil fuels is newly introduced to the global carbon cycle due to its extractive origin and 100% of the emissions from fossil jet fuel is newly introduce
  • Whereas SAF carbon sources are from carbon captured, as waste, or as residual biomass, such as used cooking oil or crop residues. These carbon sources are already present or would otherwise release into the atmosphere as they degrade.
  • The result of SAF use is a significantly reduced amount of ADDITIONAL carbon introduced into the global CARBON CYCLE.

    WHAT IS #NETZERO?
  • Net zero means achieving a balance between the GHG put into the atmosphere and that taken out.
  • Reaching net-zero applies the following principle: newly introduced carbon emissions from fossil jet fuel + reduction in the amount of additional carbon into the global carbon cycle = ZERO
  • This state is also referred to as carbon neutral.
  • Note - Zero Emissions and Zero Carbon are different, as they mean that no emissions were produced in the first place.
  • Sustainable fuel is any fuel that has the potential to generate lower carbon emissions than conventional kerosene on the LIFE CYCLE BASIS.

WHY IS THE AVIATION INDUSTRY BANKING ON SAF AND WHY IS IT THE KEY COMPONENT OF AVIATION INDUSTRY SUSTAINABILITY PLANS?

  • However, sustainability challenges cannot be solved through efficiencies, and in the long term and alternative fuels are necessary to make real progress for the following reasons
  • The average lifespan of a commercial airplane is around 20+ years and new aircraft entering during 2020-2030 are likely to operate beyond 2040
  • Battery and hydrogen technologies in development will be initially more suitable for smaller aircraft, such as commuters and regional jets, also battery weight doesn’t burn off the way fuel does, so the aircraft would need to carry the full load for the entire flight, requiring additional energy.
  • Hydrogen fuel cell aircraft could become feasible for short-range segment flights from 500km to 2000km in the next decade,
  • Hydrogen fuel cell aircraft for medium- and long-range flights may enter service by 2035-40.

Is SAF the only option available to decarbonize air travel at scale today and why?

  • The next-generation technologies like electric flight and hydrogen-powered propulsion are years away from the application at the scale required to achieve 50% CO2 reduction.
  • In the immediate future, SAF is not only the most achievable but also the most effective decarbonization pathway to reduce aviation lifecycle emissions. SAF remains a key component of industry sustainability plans because
  • SAF eliminates polluting emissions in the first place, at least, in theory, it has the potential to reduce lifecycle emissions between 80% to 99%, compared with conventional aviation fuel 99%, depending on the technology, feedstock, and transportation.
  • SAF is “also a drop-in fuel, which can be blended in a ratio of up to 50% with conventional jet fuel for use in aircraft operating today.
  • SAF blends are technically compatible with fuel delivery and airport fuelling infrastructure. No investments in delivery or fuelling infrastructure are needed.
  • SAF could avoid early retirement and write-off of older aircraft types as it allows all aircraft to reduce their net carbon footprint.
