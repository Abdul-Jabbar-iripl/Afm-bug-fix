Session 11.6

Hose Inspections and Testing

Hose inspection and test pressures

  • Monthly–all airport depot hoses
    1. Fully extend the hose and apply normal pump pressure to loading hoses inspect for external damage, leakage and other signs of weakness, ensuring full circumference of the hose external surface is checked
    2. Inspect for coupling slippage indicated by misalignment of the hose coupling and exposed areas where slippage has occurred
    3. release the pressure completely and inspect for soft areas especially within about 45cm (18 inches) of couplings, since these sections are particularly prone to deterioration
  • Monthly–hydrant low point servicing vehicle hoses
    1. Hydrant low point servicing vehicle hoses should be pressurised to a maximum of 80 psi (5.5 bar)
    2. Monthly testing and inspection shall be carried out as above for depot hoses
  • Six-monthly test–pressure hoses only
    1. A pressure test, using a hydrostatic test pump, shall be carried out
    1. routinely every 6 months
    2. when commissioning new hoses,
    3. whenever couplings are attached or re-attached to hoses, and
    4. when accidental damage to a hose is suspected

11.6.1 Hose Inspections and Testing

  • It is essential to use only approved combinations of hoses and couplings because of variations in hose wall thickness (generally between European and U.S.A. manufacturers)
  • European couplings shall be used with European hoses, and American couplings with American hoses
  • An exception is Gammon’s Jetcraft which is similar in wall thickness to European hoses
  • Reattachable couplings may be reused on their original hose (e,g, if hose shortening is required)
  • Only new couplings of an approved type shall be used on new hoses
  • Couplings shall be fitted to hoses in a suitably equipped workshop by competent trained staff certified by the hose manufacturer or their authorised distributor
  • Hose couplings connections shall be :
    1. For hydrant INTAKE HOSES
      1. permanent and non-re-attachable
      2. Hose couplings shall be fitted by the hose manufacturer or by a distributor who is trained by the manufacturer only
    2. For DELIVERY HOSES, platform riser hoses and low point flushing hoses
      1. may be fitted with either permanent, non-reattachable couplings or re-attachable couplings
  • The couplings on all product hoses shall be fabricated of NON-SPARKING metal
  • Couplings shall be standard male NPT/BSP screw couplings
  • Under no circumstances should hoses using “BANDED” connections be used in aviation service
  • All fuelling and fueller loading hose shall be one continuous length of heavy duty Grade 2, Type C
  • Defuelling hoses shall be Type F for use on dedicated defuelling equipment
  • Bridger and rail car discharge applications may use hose complying with BS 3492 Type BX Class 1
  • Hydrant intake hoses maybe 4-inch ID and shall be wrapped around the vehicle
  • The hose shall be fitted with castor assemblies incorporating lifting handles at regular intervals to avoid hose contact with the ground when deployed

11.6.2 Deck Hoses

  • Deck hoses maybe 2–3 inches ID with minimum length required to reach the aircraft with fueling points from the platform and ensure that the platform's complete lowering does not load the aircraft adaptor
  • It is recommended that the deck hoses shall be fitted, so the hose rests on the platform floor to assist with reducing the load on the aircraft adapter when the platform is in the raised position
  • An exterior plastic coil wrap shall be installed where the hoses rest on the platform floor to prevent chaffing

11.6.3 Reel Hoses

  • Reel hoses shall be:
    1. Jet Fuel Underwing: 2–2 ½ inches ID
    2. Jet fuel Overwing: 1¼-1½ inch ID
    3. AVGAS: 1–1¼ inch ID
  • Hose Protection: Yellow color HDPE hose protection beads may be provided to avoid hose contact with the ground when deployed
  • Hose reel shall be suitably sized to accommodate the beads
  • Spacing of each bead on the hose is 4 feet
  • Riser hoses, when equipped, shall be 4-inch ID and designed not to allow kinking nor chaffing when in the down or stowed position

11.6.2 Hose Identification

  • Each hose assembly shall be given a permanent and unique identification number.
  • The manufacturer’s serial number molded into the hose cover is not necessarily unique.

11.6.5 Manufacturer Certificate

  • Each hose assembly shall be furnished with a manufacturer’s certificate stating that the hose has been inspected, couplings attached and hydrostatically tested in accordance with hose specifications
  • The certificate shall include the hydrostatic test pressure, hose manufacture dates, and assembly dates
