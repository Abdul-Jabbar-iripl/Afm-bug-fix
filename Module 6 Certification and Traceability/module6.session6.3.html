Session 6.3

Introduction to Traceability

    Traceability

  • Traceability for aviation turbine fuel is defined as the ability to track distinct batches of fuel through the distribution system back to the original point of manufacture.
  • This requires batch volume and quality documentation with information on additive concentration, hydro-processed content, and synthetic components (if present) to be maintained.
  • Traceability depends upon the availability and accuracy of the supply documentation; Test Certificates & Release Certificates

    6.3.1 Challenges and Importance of traceability

  • Traceability may get lost or is compromised by:
    • Complex and diverse trading systems
    • Co-mingling of products at Terminals
    • Floating storage systems
    • Ship-to-ship transfers
    • Poor documentation
    • Ignorance
    • Carelessness

    Importance of Traceability

  • Traceability
    • This means that fuel is traceable back to its manufacturing source. In the event of a product quality event, it may be necessary to notify all recipients of the fuel.
    • Traceability also implies that the fuel was born as aviation fuel and remains aviation fuel through distribution.
      • For example, kerosene should not be re-branded as jet fuel during distribution.
    • Traceability is a CRITICAL component of INCIDENT INVESTIGATION
    • Traceability:
      • Traceability:
      • Along all logistic and distribution chains
      • All the way back to the point of Manufacture
      • Ensures Additives are not overdosed downstream in the Supply chain
      • Provides confidence that ALL Specification requirements have been met.
    • Homogeneity:
      • Density does not vary by more than +/- 3.0 kg/m3
    • Line clear (or line content) / Residual Heel:
      • Less than 3.0% of the Tank Volume
    • Traceability is a requirement of the Def Stan Annex D, EI/JIG 1530
    • ASTM D1655 Annex X3 gives ‘Guidance’ on Traceability

    Traceability Problems

  • Be Alert, as
    • sometimes, the Certificates and other documents are not worth the paper that they are written on
    • don’t take false assurance from documents alone
    • there could be breaks in the document trail
    • take particular care with new supply routes
